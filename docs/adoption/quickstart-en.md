# habitable — quick start (one page, print me)

A friendly guide to documenting a housing problem — mold, no heat, pests, water, electrical,
or something broken — so you have a strong record. Your photos stay **on your phone**, locked
with your passphrase. **No account. No company can read them.**

> **Please know:** this tool is still being tested (it's called *alpha*). It is great for
> practice, but **do not rely on it for a real legal matter yet.** It gives you good
> documentation — it is **not** legal advice. Ask your tenant lawyer or legal-aid group what
> to do with it.

## 1. Get the app on your phone

Your organizer will give you a web address. Open it in your phone's browser, then:

- **Android (Chrome):** tap the ⋮ menu → **Add to Home screen**.
- **iPhone (Safari):** tap Share → **Add to Home Screen**.

It gets its own icon, like any app. You don't make an account. You **do** pick a
**passphrase** — choose a strong one and **write it down somewhere safe** (see step 4).

## 2. Take a picture of the problem

1. Start a new issue. Pick what it is (mold, heat, pests…) and the room.
2. Take a photo, or add one you already have.
3. Add a short note and the date things happened (a repair you asked for, the landlord's
   reply or silence).

The moment you take the photo, your phone **locks it as-is and gives it a fingerprint** —
right away, even with no internet. That's what later proves it wasn't changed. Take pictures
each time the problem comes back; it builds your timeline.

**You can't break this by tapping around.** Explore freely.

## 3. What the words mean (plain version)

- **Awaiting timestamp** — *This is normal, not a problem.* Your photo is already safe. The
  app just hasn't added the official date-stamp yet, because that needs internet. Next time
  you have Wi-Fi or signal, it adds it on its own. Being offline for days is fine.
- **Timestamped** — the date-stamp is on. Your photo now has proof of *when* it existed.
- **Chain intact / verified** — the record checks out: nothing was altered after you took it.
- **Packet** — the file you (or your organizer) can hand to a court or inspector. Anyone can
  check it themselves; they don't have to take your word for it. Anything you share has your
  **location removed**.

## 4. Keep your evidence safe

- **Write down your passphrase** by hand and keep it somewhere safe — not stuck to the phone.
- **Ask your organizer to help you make a recovery backup.** It's a separate safety copy with
  its own password. A trusted organizer can hold it for you.
- A **second person's phone** (your organizer's) can keep a synced copy, so a lost or broken
  phone doesn't lose your case.

## ⚠️ The one big warning

**If you forget your passphrase, and there's no recovery backup, and no other phone has your
case — your evidence is gone. For good. No one can get it back — not even the people who made
this.**

That's on purpose: it's the same reason a landlord, a hacker, or a court order can't get your
photos either. So please, today: **write down your passphrase and make a backup.**
